Identifying potential KRAS inhibitors through computational methods

Project Overview

KRAS mutations are found in approximately 90% of pancreatic cancers, making it a highly attractive but challenging drug target. This project aimed to identify potential small molecule inhibitors through virtual screening of a compound library against the KRAS G12D mutant protein.

The workflow combined structure-based virtual screening with ligand-based approaches to prioritize compounds for experimental validation.

Workflow

Target Preparation

KRAS G12D mutant structure (PDB: 4EPV) was prepared by adding hydrogens, assigning charges, and defining the binding site around the switch II pocket.

Ligand Library

A library of 20,000 drug-like compounds from ZINC database was filtered for Lipinski rule compliance and prepared using RDKit.

Virtual Screening

Two-stage screening using PyRx: rapid screening with AutoDock Vina (top 500 hits), followed by more exhaustive docking of top candidates.

Analysis & Visualization

Binding poses and interactions were analyzed using PyMOL. Compounds were prioritized based on binding energy, interaction patterns, and drug-likeness.

Key Results

Top Hits Identified

5 compounds showed binding energies < -9.0 kcal/mol and formed hydrogen bonds with key residues (D69, Y71).

Structural Insights

Identified a common pharmacophore with hydrophobic groups targeting the switch II pocket and polar interactions with D69.

Impact & Next Steps

This computational screening identified promising KRAS inhibitors that are now being experimentally validated by our collaborators in the biochemistry department. The structural insights gained are also informing structure-based optimization of existing KRAS inhibitors.

Future work will include molecular dynamics simulations to assess binding stability and free energy calculations to improve binding affinity predictions.
